I am a research scientist at Stanford University in the W. W. Hansen Experimental Physics Laboratory, where I study the Sun and space weather as a member of the NASA Solar Dynamics Observatory science team. I previously worked at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics, where I studied solar flares as a member of two NASA Heliophysics missions called TRACE and Hinode.

My research focuses on analyzing large data sets, on the scale of terabytes to petabytes, that describe the Sun and space weather, and I authored a book on the subject titled Machine Learning, Statistics, and Data Mining for Heliophysics. I also serve as Vice Chair of the Advisory Board for the SunPy Project, which develops a Python-based ecosystem of open source scientific software for data analysis in solar physics, and the heliophysics Topic Editor for the Journal of Open Source Software (JOSS). I am also a Contributing Editor at Sky & Telescope magazine, covering topics related to the Sun and Sun-like stars.
