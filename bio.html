I have 13 years of experience working in space science. For the last 11 years, I studied the Sun and space weather as a research scientist at Stanford University in the W. W. Hansen Experimental Physics Laboratory. My research focused on predicting solar flares by analyzing millions of images of the Sun, taken by the NASA Solar Dynamics Observatory, with machine learning algorithms. I also authored a book titled Machine Learning, Statistics, and Data Mining for Heliophysics.

I previously worked at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics on two NASA missions called TRACE and Hinode.

I am committed to the open source scientific software community as Vice Chair of the Advisory Board for the SunPy Project and Data Science Editor for the Journal of Open Source Software (JOSS). I also write popular science articles as a Contributing Editor at Sky & Telescope magazine.
